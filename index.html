AIRMM — Artificial Intelligence Resilience Maturity Model

v0.1 • 19 Jul 2025

Build AI‑aware organizational resilience, step by step

AI‑RMM (Artificial Intelligence Resilience Maturity Model) is a practical framework that helps organizations assess, measure, and improve resilience around AI systems. It aligns with the NIST AI RMF core functions — Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage — and structures progress across five maturity levels from Initial to Optimizing.

What is AIRMM?

A maturity pathway for organizations to anticipate, withstand, recover from, and adapt to AI‑related disruptions. It distinguishes between “resilient AI systems” and “organizations resilient to AI” by focusing on AI‑Aware Organizational Resilience—policies, governance, monitoring, workforce, and operational safeguards around AI usage.

Two‑digit versioning

Documents use a simple d.d scheme (e.g., 0.1). Minor updates accrue until they roll into the next major (e.g., 0.9 → 1.0). Clarity over complexity.

Major (X.0): structural/strategic changes.
Minor (0.Y): incremental refinements.

Five maturity levels

1 • Initial 2 • Managed 3 • Defined 4 • Quantitatively Managed 5 • Optimizing

Each level bundles concrete controls—from ad‑hoc awareness through standardized processes, measurable performance, and continuous improvement.

NIST AI RMF alignment

Govern: Policies, accountability, culture, stakeholder engagement, third‑party management.
Map: Context, categorization, capabilities & scope, component risks, societal impacts.
Measure: Methods & metrics, trustworthy characteristics, risk tracking, measurement feedback.
Manage: Prioritization & response, benefit maximization, third‑party risk, treatment & communication.

Why it matters

AIRMM helps keep humans firmly in control—establishing guardrails, monitoring, and “kill‑switch” style disengagement for AI that goes off‑spec.

GOVERN • sample sub‑categories & level cues

Policies & Compliance (Govern 1.1): from identifying applicable AI laws ➜ standardized, KPI‑tracked compliance ➜ proactive policy updates.
Trustworthy AI Integration (Govern 1.2): define & embed principles ➜ assess adherence with metrics ➜ continuously refresh to new risks.
Accountability Structures (Govern 2.x): clarify roles & training ➜ measure effectiveness ➜ refine structures over time.

MAP • foundation before risk treatment

Define purpose, scope, users, deployment contexts, and human oversight; document limitations and TEVV.

Context (Map 1.1): intended use, user expectations, settings, metrics.
Categorization (Map 2.1): tasks/techniques, specs, oversight.
Capabilities/Benefits/Costs (Map 3.x): value vs. non‑monetary costs; training & proficiency; scope drift checks.

MEASURE • trustworthy characteristics & tracking

Methods & Metrics (Measure 1.1): select, implement, and document; integrate into lifecycle.
Safety, Security, Fairness, Privacy (Measure 2.x): define metrics, test, monitor in production, and iterate.
Risk Tracking (Measure 3.x): handle emergent risks and feedback loops; promote risk culture.

MANAGE • operationalize decisions

Prioritization & Response (Manage 1.x): gap analysis, mitigation plans, trade‑offs, residual risk documentation.
Benefit Maximization (Manage 2.x): maintain performance, consider non‑AI alternatives, train, and define disengagement criteria.
Third‑party & Communication (Manage 3–4): supply‑chain oversight; post‑deployment monitoring; incident response & transparency.
